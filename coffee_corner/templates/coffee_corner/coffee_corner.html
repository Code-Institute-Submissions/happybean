Aeropress
  • schedule Brewing Time: 2min
  • coffee_maker Grind Type: Ground
  • water_drop Water: 200ml
Steps:
  1. Insert filter into holder and lock in place
  2. Rinse brewer and filter paper with hot water
  3. Sit the main part of the Aeropress on a cup
  4. Add ground coffee
  5. Add hot water
  6. Stir coffee
  7. Put press in place, seal to create a vacuum
  8. Wait one minute
  9. Push the plunger down until coffee is poured
  10. Pull press up one inch
  11. Pull press up slightly
Chemex
  • schedule Brewing Time: 8min
  • coffee_maker Grind Type: Ground
  • water_drop Water: 1 litre
Steps:
  1. Boil water
  2. Rinse filter and brewer with hot water
  3. Add ground coffee to filter
  4. Use pouring or regular kettle
  5. Pour slowly, enough to saturate the coffee
  6. Wait as water travels through the coffee bed
  7. Pour again with slow, circular movements
  8. Let sit until all water has filtered through
  9. Remove filter, coffee is ready to pour
Espresso
  • schedule Brewing Time: 20-30 seconds
  • coffee_maker Grind Type: Beans or Ground
  • water_drop Water: Fill the coffee machine's water tank
Steps:
  1. Depending on the machine, either pour beans or ground at the top or fill your portafilter basket to the top with ground coffee
  2. If you are using the portafilter basket, using a tamper, apply even pressure to the coffee grounds to ensure it’s level and place your portafilter into the espresso machine’s group head
  3. Start the water flowing by pushing the button, or switching on your espresso machine
Frenchpress
  • schedule Brewing Time: 3.5 min
  • coffee_maker Grind Type: Ground
  • water_drop Water: 30g coffee to 500ml water
Steps:
  1. Preheat the press with hot water and discard
  2. Add coarsely ground coffee to the press and cover the coffee with water to bloom, allow to sit for 30secs
  3. Stir the coffee twice and pour the remainder of the water into the press
  4. Leave to brew for 3.5 minutes
  5. Scoop out any floating grounds of coffee, and throw them away
  6. Place the filter on top of the press and gently push it down
Moka Pot
  • schedule Brewing Time: 8 min
  • coffee_maker Grind Type: Ground
  • water_drop Water: Fill lower chamber
Steps:
  1. Unscrew the top from the safety bottom
  2. Remove the brew basket
  3. Add hot water to just below safety valve
  4. Add ground coffee to brew basket
  5. Ensure coffee is level, no need to pack it
  6. Reassemble brewer
  7. With lid open, place pot on heat (low-med)
  8. Pressure forces coffee into the upper chamber
  9. As coffee pales and bubbles, move from the heat
V60
  • schedule Brewing Time: 20 seconds
  • coffee_maker Grind Type: Ground
  • water_drop Water: 250g
Steps:
  1. Position V60 over the cup or glass beaker
  2. Place the filter paper in the V60, rinse the paper with hot water and empty the cup.
  3. Put the coffee into the V60 and level the coffee
  4. Slowly pour the hot water into the centre of the coffee, avoid pouring water on the paper directly

How to Store Coffee Beans
The basics of storing your fresh coffee beans:
  • Decrease air movement
  • Keep the ambient temperature fairly consistent
  • Keep beans well away from moisture
  • Limit beans exposure to light.
Why the grind matters
Grind size affects contact time, extraction rate, and flow rate of your brewing:
  • Extraction rates of coffee grounds increase with a larger surface area
  • To increase surface area, grind your coffee finer
  • The higher the extraction rate, the less contact time needed
  • Finer grinds reduce the flow rate of water, increasing contact time
Flavour Coffee with Spices
Good coffee has its natural, often very complex sensory profile however if you are looking to spice up your coffee, here are our recommendations:

  • Coffee with vanilla: If you have a sweet tooth and appreciate mild flavours, choose vanilla. The inconspicuous vanilla pod hides wonderful, balsamic seeds that will emphasize the caramel and almond aftertaste, as well as subtly tweak the natural sweetness of the brew. What’s more, vanilla complements the other spices perfectly, balancing them and creating a sweet and spicy indulgence for the senses.
  • Coffee with cinnamon: A pinch of cinnamon in coffee will enrich the drink with its characteristic sweetness combined with a delicate, pleasantly warming spiciness. You can use ground cinnamon or cinnamon sticks, which will additionally strengthen the visual appeal of the beverage.
  • Coffee with cardamom With its subtle, warm aroma and an original camphor-lemon aftertaste, which is an interesting match for coffees with a fruit flavour profile.
  • Coffee with star anise: It has a very distinct aroma, and is quite spicy with a noticeable herbal aftertaste similar to liquorice. You can choose ground anise or pods in the shape of stars.
